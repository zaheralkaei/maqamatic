About Maqamatic

Maqamatic is a generative music tool for creating melodies in the Arabic Maqam tradition.

Features

  • 22 Maqamat - Complete modal definitions with quarter-tone support
  • 20 Ajnas - The building blocks of maqam scales
  • 38 Iqa'at - Traditional rhythmic cycles
  • Intelligent Generation - Uses sayr (melodic path) rules and transition matrices
  • 24+ Parameters - Full control over melody, form, rhythm, modulation, and expression

How It Works

The generator follows traditional rules for melodic motion, phrase structure, and maqam modulation, creating authentic-sounding melodies while allowing creative exploration through 24+ adjustable parameters.

Understanding Maqam

A maqam (plural: maqamat) is a melodic mode used in Arabic music. Each maqam defines:

  • Scale - The specific pitches used, including quarter tones
  • Ajnas - Component tetrachords/pentachords
  • Important Degrees - Notes that receive special emphasis
  • Sayr - The characteristic melodic path and phrases

Understanding Iqa'

An iqa' (plural: iqa'at) is a rhythmic cycle providing the metric framework:

  • Dum - Low, resonant stroke (bass)
  • Tak - High, sharp stroke (treble)
  • Ka - Light filler stroke

Parameter Guide

Tradition
Controls how strictly the generator follows classical maqam rules.
Energy
Overall intensity. Low = calm, high = agitated.
Density
Note density per phrase. Sparse = contemplative, dense = lively.
Motion Type
Balance between stepwise motion and larger interval leaps.
Musical Form
Structure: free, binary (AB), ternary (ABA), rondo (ABACA).
Ornaments
Amount of melodic decoration like trills and mordents.
Modulation
How often and how far the melody shifts to related maqamat.
Pitch Gravity
How strongly important scale degrees attract the melody.
