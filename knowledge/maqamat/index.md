# Maqam Encyclopedia

## Overview

A **maqam** (plural: maqamat) is a system of melodic modes used in Arabic music. Each maqam defines:
- A specific scale with characteristic intervals (including microtones)
- A set of rules for melodic development (sayr)
- Characteristic phrases and ornaments
- Emotional qualities (tab')
- Relationships to other maqamat through modulation

## The 24-TET Quarter-Tone System

Arabic music uses quarter-tones (half of a Western semitone). In 24-tone equal temperament:
- Each octave = 24 quarter-tones
- Semitone = 2 quarter-tones (200 cents)
- Quarter-tone = 1 unit (50 cents)

### Interval Notation
| Symbol | Name | Quarter-tones | Cents |
|--------|------|---------------|-------|
| m2 | Minor second | 2 | 100 |
| N2 | Neutral second | 3 | 150 |
| M2 | Major second | 4 | 200 |
| m3 | Minor third | 6 | 300 |
| N3 | Neutral third | 7 | 350 |
| M3 | Major third | 8 | 400 |
| P4 | Perfect fourth | 10 | 500 |
| A4 | Augmented fourth | 12 | 600 |
| P5 | Perfect fifth | 14 | 700 |

### Accidentals
| Symbol | Name | Effect |
|--------|------|--------|
| ♯ | Sharp | Raise by 2 quarter-tones (100 cents) |
| ♭ | Flat | Lower by 2 quarter-tones (100 cents) |
| 𝄲 | Half-sharp | Raise by 1 quarter-tone (50 cents) |
| 𝄳 | Half-flat | Lower by 1 quarter-tone (50 cents) |

## Maqam Structure

Each maqam consists of **ajnas** (singular: jins) - tetrachords or pentachords that combine to form the complete scale.

### Jins Types
- **Tetrachord**: 4-note unit spanning a perfect fourth
- **Pentachord**: 5-note unit spanning a perfect fifth

### Key Positions
- **Tonic (Qarar)**: The fundamental note, center of gravity
- **Dominant (Ghammaz)**: Secondary important note, often starting point of upper jins
- **Leading tone**: Note that resolves to tonic in cadences

## Modulation (Intiqal)

Modulation between maqamat is a sophisticated art. Common transitions:

### From Rast
- Rast → Bayati (via shared D)
- Rast → Nahawand (changing 3rd degree)
- Rast → Sikah (via E𝄳)
- Rast → Hijaz (on the 4th degree)

### From Bayati
- Bayati → Rast (raising the 2nd degree)
- Bayati → Saba (lowering 4th degree)
- Bayati → Hijaz (on same tonic)

### From Hijaz
- Hijaz → Bayati (naturalizing the 2nd)
- Hijaz → Nahawand (on 4th degree)
- Hijaz → Kurd (flattening 3rd)
