# Jins Upper Hijaz

## Overview

Jins Upper Hijaz is a 4-note jins. Like other "upper" ajnas, its tonic is the last degree in the scale rather than the first. It is the upper jins in many maqamat that use Hijaz on the lower jins and a different upper jins — but in Maqam Hijaz, it serves as the upper register of the maqam itself.

---

## Scale (on G to C)

```
(Baggage)  Ghammaz                    Tonic
    ↓         ↓                          ↓
  (F) ------ G -------- A♭ ------- B -------- C ------ (D)
         M2        m2          A2         m2
        (200¢)   (100¢)      (300¢)     (100¢)
```

Note: Unlike most ajnas, the tonic is at the TOP of the scale.

## Interval Structure

- **Total span**: Perfect 4th (500¢) from ghammaz to tonic
- **Pattern**: m2 - A2 - m2 (100¢ - 300¢ - 100¢)
- **Character**: Exotic, dramatic, Middle Eastern (augmented 2nd)

## Distinctive Features

- **Inverted structure**: Tonic at the top, ghammaz at the bottom
- Same intervals as regular Jins Hijaz but with reversed melodic gravity
- The augmented second (A♭ to B) creates the same "exotic" Middle Eastern character as standard Hijaz
- The melody typically ascends through the augmented second toward the upper tonic for resolution

## Usage

- **Upper jins** in maqamat that combine Hijaz-family sounds in the upper register
- Used as the upper jins in Maqam Hijaz (in addition to its role in other maqamat)
- Creates the same dramatic flavor as standard Hijaz but resolved upward
