# Jins Iraq

## Overview

Jins Iraq is a 4-note jins. It is the first jins in Maqam Iraq. Its defining characteristic is the half-flat tonic (B half-flat) combined with the half-flat ghammaz (E half-flat), giving it the same "between" quality that the Sikah family is known for, but with a D natural at the 3rd degree rather than the D half-flat of the wider Iraq-family scale.

In this project's data, the jins is the first four notes of Maqam Iraq's scale (B half-flat, C, D natural, E half-flat), with intervals N2 - M2 - N2 (1.5, 2, 1.5 semitones).

## Distinctive Features

- The half-flat tonic (B half-flat) connects it to the Sikah family
- Two half-flats (Bрқ„і, Eрқ„і) frame the jins, with a D natural in the middle
- The D natural at degree 3 distinguishes this jins from the fully-symmetrical Вҫ Вҫ Вҫ "true" Iraq conceptualization that some musicological sources describe
- Strongly associated with Sufi music and spiritual contexts
- The M2 in the middle (C to D) creates a sense of expansion before the final N2 settles back to the half-flat ghammaz
