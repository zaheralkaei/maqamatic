# The Jins System (Ajnas)

## Overview

The **Jins** (plural: **Ajnas**) is a maqam scale fragment of 3, 4, or 5 notes. The jins is the basic melodic unit in Arabic music, as a maqam is really a pathway among many ajnas. Each jins is defined by its intervals, which don't change when it is transposed and give it its distinct and recognizable character.

## Key Concepts

### Tonic
The **tonic** of a jins is the note of principal melodic emphasis, the note that the melody returns to for resolution. This is usually the first note in the jins scale.

### Leading Tone
The **leading tone** is the note immediately below the tonic of a jins.

### Ghammaz
The **ghammaz** is the most important note of melodic emphasis (besides the tonic), as well as the most common starting point of a new jins. Changing to a new jins or maqam is called **modulation**.

## Jins Size and Scale Structure

### Size
The **size** of a jins is the number of notes between the tonic and the ghammaz. These notes are called the jins' **basic scale** (or simply "scale").

### Basic Scale
The core notes from tonic to ghammaz that define the jins.

### Jins Baggage
Outside the basic scale (on either side) are notes that are part and parcel of the jins' melodic vocabulary. These notes are called the **jins baggage**.

### Extended Scale
The **basic scale** plus the **jins baggage** make up the jins' **extended scale**.

## Notation Convention

In notation:
- **Basic scale** notes are shown as large notes
- **Tonic** and **ghammaz** are shown as whole notes
- **Jins baggage** notes are shown in smaller size
- Some ajnas include multiple options for certain notes in the jins baggage

## Relationship to Maqam

A **maqam** is constructed as a pathway through multiple ajnas. Understanding the individual ajnas and how they connect is essential to understanding maqam structure and melodic development (sayr).
