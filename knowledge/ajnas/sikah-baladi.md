# Jins Sikah Baladi

## Overview

Jins Sikah Baladi has no ghammaz, and therefore no defined size. Its melody is centered around its tonic. This jins is very difficult to accurately notate; instead its intervals and intonation are best learned by ear.

## Interval Structure

- **No ghammaz**: Melody centers around the tonic
- **No defined size**: Due to lack of ghammaz
- **Pattern**: Features multiple neutral seconds and half-flat/half-sharp notes
- **Character**: Deeply folk/rural, earthy, microtonal

## Distinctive Features

- Contains **multiple quarter-tone inflections** (E𝄳, F𝄲, A𝄳, B𝄳)
- The F half-sharp (F𝄲) is unusual and gives it a distinctive rural character
- **Best learned by ear** - notation is only an approximation
- The name "Baladi" means "folk" or "rural/country" - indicating its traditional, earthy character
- Three consecutive neutral seconds in the approach to and from the tonic

## Performance Note

The intervals in Jins Sikah Baladi are particularly fluid and expressive. Performers often bend and inflect these notes in ways that defy precise notation. The written intervals should be considered approximate guides rather than fixed values.

## Usage

- Occurs on the **octave of Maqam Sikah Baladi**
- Expresses a deep, folk-rooted, earthy quality
- Carries the characteristic Sikah ethereal quality but with a more grounded, traditional flavor
