# Jins Upper Nahawand

## Overview

Jins Upper Nahawand is a 4-note jins. Like other "upper" ajnas, its tonic is the last degree in the scale rather than the first. It is used as the upper jins in maqamat that have Nahawand-family sounds in the lower register, particularly Maqam Nahawand itself and several maqamat that modulate through Nahawand.

---

## Scale (on G to C)

```
(Baggage)  Ghammaz                    Tonic
    ↓         ↓                          ↓
  (F) ------ G -------- A -------- B♭ ------- C ------ (D)
         M2        M2         m2         M2
        (200¢)   (200¢)    (100¢)    (200¢)
```

Note: Unlike most ajnas, the tonic is at the TOP of the scale.

## Interval Structure

- **Total span**: Perfect 4th (500¢) from ghammaz to tonic
- **Pattern**: M2 - M2 - m2 (200¢ - 200¢ - 100¢)
- **Character**: Minor quality, soft, natural-minor-like

## Distinctive Features

- **Inverted structure**: Tonic at the top, ghammaz at the bottom
- Same intervals as regular Jins Nahawand but with reversed melodic gravity
- Familiar to Western ears due to similarity to the natural minor mode
- The melody typically ascends toward the tonic for resolution

## Generator Notes

- **Tonic (degree 4)**: hard
- **Ghammaz (degree 1)**: hard
- **Emphasis**: [1, 3, 4]
- **Must include**: [1, 4]
- **Resolution target**: 1
- **Entry notes**: [4, 3]
- **Exit notes**: [1]
- **Behavior**: descending, range 1–4

## Usage

- **Upper jins** in Maqam Nahawand and related maqamat
- Common as the upper jins in many maqamat of the Nahawand family
- Used when the melody needs to resolve upward to a higher tonic
- Creates a sense of ascent and arrival at the upper tonic
