# Iqa' Malfuf 2/4

**Malfuf** (Malfūf) — 2/4, 4 eighths per measure.

## Overview

> Iqa‘ Malfuf is a member of the 2/4 iqa‘at family whose feel is described as "rolling" because of its quick succession of dum and tak. It modulates often to and from Maqsum.
>
> — [MaqamWorld](https://www.maqamworld.com/en/iqaa/malfuf.php)

---

## Pattern

**Notation:** `D - T T`  
**Base unit:** eighth | **Total divisions:** 16 (per measure)

**Legend:** `D` = Dum (bass, accent 2) | `T` = Tak (sharp, accent 1) | `k` = Ka (ghost note, no accent) | `-` = Rest

**Events (position, duration, accent):**

```
D(0,4,2) -(4,4,0) T(8,4,1) T(12,4,1)
```

## Tempo

- **Typical:** 140 BPM
- **Range:** 100–180 BPM

## Feel

Rolling, continuous.

## Usage

- **Genres:** pop, folk, dance
- **Regions:** Egypt, Levant

## MIDI

- **Dum (D):** note 36 (C2)
- **Tak (T):** note 42 (F#2)
- **Ka (k):** note 39 (D#2)
