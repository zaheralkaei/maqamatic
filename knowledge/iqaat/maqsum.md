# Iqa' Maqsum 4/4

**Maqsum** (Maqsūm) — 4/4, 8 eighths per measure.

## Overview

> Iqa‘ Maqsum is by far the most popular and widely-used iqa‘ in Arabic music. It is the principal iqa‘ used in the Tarab genre of the twentieth century. Maqsum modulates very often to and from other 4/4 iqa‘at like Baladi, Wahda, and Wahda w-Nuss.
>
> — [MaqamWorld](https://www.maqamworld.com/en/iqaa/maqsum.php)

---

## Pattern

**Notation:** `D T - T D - T -`  
**Base unit:** eighth | **Total divisions:** 32 (per measure)

**Legend:** `D` = Dum (bass, accent 2) | `T` = Tak (sharp, accent 1) | `k` = Ka (ghost note, no accent) | `-` = Rest

**Events (position, duration, accent):**

```
D(0,4,2) T(4,4,1) -(8,4,0) T(12,4,1) D(16,4,2) -(20,4,0) T(24,4,1) -(28,4,0)
```

## Tempo

- **Typical:** 110 BPM
- **Range:** 80–140 BPM

## Feel

Foundational, driving.

## Usage

- **Genres:** classical, pop, folk
- **Regions:** all Arab world

## Form grammar

Main iqa' for the **Bashraf** form (instrumental).

## MIDI

- **Dum (D):** note 36 (C2)
- **Tak (T):** note 42 (F#2)
- **Ka (k):** note 39 (D#2)
