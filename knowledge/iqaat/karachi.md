# Iqa' Karachi 2/4

**Karachi** (Karachī) — 2/4, 4 eighths per measure.

## Overview

> Iqa‘ Karachi is a member of the 2/4 iqa‘at family that has a lazy feel and can be played at relatively slow tempos. Although it modulates often to and from Malfuf, Karachi does not share its rapid and urgent feel.
>
> — [MaqamWorld](https://www.maqamworld.com/en/iqaa/karachi.php)

---

## Pattern

**Notation:** `D T D T`  
**Base unit:** eighth | **Total divisions:** 16 (per measure)

**Legend:** `D` = Dum (bass, accent 2) | `T` = Tak (sharp, accent 1) | `k` = Ka (ghost note, no accent) | `-` = Rest

**Events (position, duration, accent):**

```
D(0,4,2) T(4,4,1) D(8,4,1) T(12,4,1)
```

## Tempo

- **Typical:** 130 BPM
- **Range:** 100–160 BPM

## Feel

Simple, driving, pop.

## Usage

- **Genres:** pop, modern
- **Regions:** Egypt, Levant

## MIDI

- **Dum (D):** note 36 (C2)
- **Tak (T):** note 42 (F#2)
- **Ka (k):** note 39 (D#2)
