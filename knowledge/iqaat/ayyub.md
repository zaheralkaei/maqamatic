# Iqa' Ayyub 2/4

**Ayyub** (Ayyūb) — 2/4, 4 eighths per measure.

## Overview

> Iqa‘ Ayyub is an energetic 2/4 iqa‘ from the Levant, often used in dabke line dances and zaffa (wedding processions).
>
> — [MaqamWorld](https://www.maqamworld.com/en/iqaa/ayyub.php)

---

## Pattern

**Notation:** `D k D T`  
**Base unit:** eighth | **Total divisions:** 16 (per measure)

**Legend:** `D` = Dum (bass, accent 2) | `T` = Tak (sharp, accent 1) | `k` = Ka (ghost note, no accent) | `-` = Rest

**Events (position, duration, accent):**

```
D(0,2,2) k(2,2,0) D(4,4,1) T(8,4,2) -(12,4,0)
```

## Tempo

- **Typical:** 150 BPM
- **Range:** 120–200 BPM

## Feel

Energetic, driving.

## Usage

- **Genres:** dabke, folk, zaffa
- **Regions:** Levant

## MIDI

- **Dum (D):** note 36 (C2)
- **Tak (T):** note 42 (F#2)
- **Ka (k):** note 39 (D#2)
