# Iqa' Masmoudi Kabir 4/4

**Masmoudi Kabir** (Masmūdī Kabīr) — 4/4, 16 eighths per measure.

## Overview

> Iqa‘ Masmudi Kabir (or Masmudi, for short) is one of the most popular rhythms in the Tarab repertoire of the twentieth century. The word "kabir" means great/large in Arabic, to distinguish it from Masmudi Saghir (small Masmudi, more commonly called Baladi).
>
> — [MaqamWorld](https://www.maqamworld.com/en/iqaa/masmudi_kabir.php)

---

## Pattern

**Notation:** `D D - - T - - - D - - T D - T -`  
**Base unit:** eighth | **Total divisions:** 64 (per measure)

**Legend:** `D` = Dum (bass, accent 2) | `T` = Tak (sharp, accent 1) | `k` = Ka (ghost note, no accent) | `-` = Rest

**Events (position, duration, accent):**

```
D(0,4,2) D(4,4,1) -(8,4,0) -(12,4,0) T(16,4,1) -(20,4,0) -(24,4,0) -(28,4,0) D(32,4,2) -(36,4,0) -(40,4,0) T(44,4,1) D(48,4,1) -(52,4,0) T(56,4,1) -(60,4,0)
```

## Tempo

- **Typical:** 80 BPM
- **Range:** 60–100 BPM

## Feel

Heavy, majestic, classical.

## Usage

- **Genres:** classical, belly dance
- **Regions:** Egypt

## MIDI

- **Dum (D):** note 36 (C2)
- **Tak (T):** note 42 (F#2)
- **Ka (k):** note 39 (D#2)
